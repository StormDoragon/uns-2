Overwhelmed · Qur’an 54:10

When You Are Overpowered

A prayer for the moment your strength is gone and only Allah’s help can carry you.

The dua

أَنِّي مَغْلُوبٌ فَانْتَصِرْ

Annī maghlūbun fantaṣir

“I am overpowered, so help me.”

Qur’an 54:10

The Relatable Pain

There is a kind of tiredness that does not ask for motivation. It asks for rescue. You have tried explaining, trying again, staying patient, taking the next step, and still the weight keeps pressing down.

At some point, the most truthful sentence is not long. It is simply: I am overpowered.

The Dark Place

Nuh عليه السلام called his people for a long time. He carried the burden of rejection, mockery, and isolation while remaining faithful to the message Allah gave him. The pressure was not a passing mood; it was a long campaign against his heart.

Then he called upon his Lord with words stripped of ornament: أَنِّي مَغْلُوبٌ فَانْتَصِرْ. I am overcome, so grant victory. No performance. No polished explanation. Just need, placed before power.

The Turning Point

This dua gives permission to stop pretending you are stronger than you are. It is not a failure of faith to admit exhaustion to Allah. In fact, it may be one of the clearest forms of faith: knowing exactly where help comes from.

The word maghlūb carries the feeling of being outmatched. The dua answers that feeling by turning to the One who is never outmatched.

The Closure

Allah answered Nuh عليه السلام in a way no human force could prevent. The story reminds us that when Allah supports a servant, the scale of opposition is not the deciding factor.

When you are out of arguments, out of energy, and out of options, let this dua be enough. Say it from the floor if you have to: My Lord, I am overpowered, so help me.

The takeaway for you

Admitting weakness to Allah is not defeat; it is the beginning of being carried.

Give the words space. Read them once for meaning, once for need, and once as a quiet return to Allah.
