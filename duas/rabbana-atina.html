World & Hereafter · Qur’an 2:201

Good in This Life and the Next

A prayer for the person who wants mercy without becoming narrow in what they ask.

The dua

رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ

Rabbanā ātinā fid-dunyā ḥasanah wa fil-ākhirati ḥasanah wa qinā ‘adhāban-nār

“Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire.”

Qur’an 2:201

The Relatable Pain

Some people feel guilty asking Allah for worldly ease. Others become so consumed with immediate needs that the Hereafter becomes distant in their asking. The heart can swing between shame and forgetfulness.

This dua gathers the whole human being back into balance. You are allowed to need good here, and you are created for good beyond here.

The Dark Place

Life pulls attention in fragments: health, money, family, decisions, safety, forgiveness, death, resurrection. A scattered heart may not know how to ask for all of it without becoming overwhelmed.

So Allah teaches a comprehensive request: good in the dunya, good in the akhirah, and protection from the Fire. It is short enough for the tongue and wide enough for a lifetime.

The Turning Point

The word ḥasanah is beautifully open. It includes what Allah knows to be truly good, not merely what the nafs mistakes for relief. In this world it may be faith, health, halal provision, a peaceful home, beneficial knowledge, or a heart that returns quickly. In the Hereafter it is the mercy and nearness every believer is desperate for.

This dua trains you not to split your life into sacred and ordinary. Your meals, work, marriage, healing, worship, death, and resurrection are all placed before one Lord.

The Closure

The dua ends by asking protection from the Fire, because every worldly good loses meaning if it leads away from Allah. Real good is not merely what feels pleasant now; real good remains good when you meet Him.

When you do not know what to ask, ask broadly and humbly. Let Allah choose the shape of ḥasanah for you in both homes.

The takeaway for you

Ask Allah for a life that is good now and still good when you stand before Him.

Give the words space. Read them once for meaning, once for need, and once as a quiet return to Allah.

Uns — A companion for return.

May Allah accept every call you make to Him.
