Steadfastness · Qur’an 3:8

Hold My Heart After Guidance

A prayer for when you know guidance is a gift and you are afraid of losing it.

The dua

رَبَّنَا لَا تُزِغْ قُلُوبَنَا بَعْدَ إِذْ هَدَيْتَنَا وَهَبْ لَنَا مِنْ لَدُنْكَ رَحْمَةً

Rabbanā lā tuzigh qulūbanā ba‘da idh hadaytanā wa hab lanā min ladunka raḥmah

“Our Lord, do not let our hearts deviate after You have guided us, and grant us mercy from Yourself.”

Qur’an 3:8

The Relatable Pain

There is a fear known by people who have tasted guidance: What if my heart changes? What if what feels clear today becomes distant tomorrow? What if I become casual with the very mercy that saved me?

This fear is not hopelessness. It is humility. It means you understand that guidance was never a trophy you earned; it was a gift Allah placed in your chest.

The Dark Place

The heart is subtle. It can be moved by praise, pain, desire, resentment, doubt, distraction, and company. A person may outwardly look stable while inwardly drifting by small degrees.

So the believers ask: رَبَّنَا لَا تُزِغْ قُلُوبَنَا بَعْدَ إِذْ هَدَيْتَنَا — Our Lord, do not let our hearts deviate after You guided us. They do not trust the heart to guard itself without Allah.

The Turning Point

The dua continues: وَهَبْ لَنَا مِنْ لَدُنْكَ رَحْمَةً — grant us mercy from Yourself. Steadfastness is not powered by self-admiration. It is sustained by mercy.

This changes the way you think about religious consistency. You still take the means: prayer, repentance, good company, knowledge, lowering the gaze, guarding the tongue. But beneath every means is a plea: O Allah, keep my heart with You.

The Closure

This dua is for the guided person who refuses to become arrogant about guidance. It is for the returning person, the student, the parent, the convert, the sinner trying again, and the worshiper who knows tomorrow is not guaranteed.

Say it when faith feels sweet, and say it when faith feels fragile. The One who guided you the first time is the One who can keep guiding you until the last breath.

The takeaway for you

The guided heart keeps asking to be guided because it knows Who is holding it.

Give the words space. Read them once for meaning, once for need, and once as a quiet return to Allah.
