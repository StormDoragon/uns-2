Trust & Courage · Qur’an 3:173

Allah Is Enough for the Frightened Heart

A remembrance for when the threat feels large and your resources feel small.

The dua

حَسْبُنَا اللَّهُ وَنِعْمَ الْوَكِيلُ

Ḥasbunallāhu wa ni‘mal-wakīl

“Allah is sufficient for us, and He is the best disposer of affairs.”

Qur’an 3:173

The Relatable Pain

Fear often arrives with a crowd of imagined outcomes. What if they overpower me? What if I lose everything? What if I cannot handle what comes next? The mind starts rehearsing disasters as if rehearsal could create safety.

This remembrance interrupts that spiral. It does not deny that the situation is serious. It simply refuses to let fear become the lord of the heart.

The Dark Place

The Qur’an records these words from believers who were told that people had gathered against them. The warning was meant to make them shrink. Instead, it increased them in faith, and they answered: حَسْبُنَا اللَّهُ وَنِعْمَ الْوَكِيلُ.

They did not say their numbers were enough, their planning was enough, or their courage was enough. They said Allah was enough, and He is the best One to entrust with every affair.

The Turning Point

Ḥasbunallāh is not passive. It is not a way to avoid responsibility. It is the sentence that puts responsibility in its proper size. You take the next lawful step, but your heart stops worshiping the step as if it controls destiny.

The wakīl is the One entrusted with the affair. When Allah is your Wakīl, the unseen details are not abandoned. They are held by the One whose knowledge misses nothing.

The Closure

The believers returned with Allah’s favor and bounty. The threat did not become their ending. Their trust became part of their protection.

When fear starts speaking loudly, answer it with these words. Repeat them until your body remembers: the situation may be bigger than me, but it is not bigger than Allah.

The takeaway for you

Do what is right, then let your heart rest with the One entrusted with the outcome.

Give the words space. Read them once for meaning, once for need, and once as a quiet return to Allah.
