Authenticity. Story. Meaning. Comfort.

Each dua includes source confidence, historical context, plain meaning, and a gentle way to live it daily.

1) Dua of Yunus عليه السلام

لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنتَ سُبْحَانَكَ إِنِّي كُنتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ

Easy meaning: There is no god but You. You are perfect. I have wronged myself.

Authenticity: Qur'an, Al-Anbiya 21:87 (highest certainty).

Story: Yunus عليه السلام made this in extreme darkness and distress, returning to Allah with humility and accountability.

Heart lesson: You can admit fault without losing hope. True return begins with honesty.

Comfort today: If you feel trapped by your own mistakes, this dua teaches that Allah's door is still open.

2) Dua of Ayyub عليه السلام

أَنِّي مَسَّنِيَ الضُّرُّ وَأَنتَ أَرْحَمُ الرَّاحِمِينَ

Easy meaning: Harm has touched me, and You are the Most Merciful.

Authenticity: Qur'an, Al-Anbiya 21:83.

Story: Ayyub عليه السلام suffered long illness and loss, yet spoke with adab, naming pain without despair.

Heart lesson: Sabr does not mean denial. You can describe pain and still trust Allah completely.

Comfort today: For long tests and chronic stress, this dua gives language to endure with dignity.

3) Hasbunallahu wa ni'mal wakeel

حَسْبُنَا اللَّهُ وَنِعْمَ الْوَكِيلُ

Easy meaning: Allah is enough for us, and He is the best Disposer of affairs.

Authenticity: Qur'an, Aal 'Imran 3:173.

Story: Said in moments of threat and fear, when believers were pressured and outnumbered.

Heart lesson: Tawakkul is active trust: do your part, then hand over outcomes to Allah.

Comfort today: When fear rises, this dhikr calms the heart by relocating control to Allah.

4) Wa ufawwidu amri ilallah

وَأُفَوِّضُ أَمْرِي إِلَى اللَّهِ

Easy meaning: I entrust my affair to Allah.

Authenticity: Qur'an, Ghafir 40:44.

Story: Spoken by the believing man in Pharaoh's court amid danger and political pressure.

Heart lesson: Entrusting to Allah is not weakness; it is moral courage with spiritual certainty.

Comfort today: For conflict and uncertainty, this phrase restores inner steadiness.

5) Rabbi inni maghloobun fantasir

رَبِّ إِنِّي مَغْلُوبٌ فَانتَصِرْ

Easy meaning: My Lord, I am overpowered, so help me.

Authenticity: Qur'an, Al-Qamar 54:10 (dua of Nuh عليه السلام).

Story: Nuh عليه السلام made this after long rejection and hostility.

Heart lesson: Brevity can be deep worship: honest weakness plus direct reliance on Allah.

Comfort today: When you feel emotionally overwhelmed, this dua gives you a truthful, simple cry for help.
